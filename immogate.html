Overview

ImmoGate is an advanced sentiment analysis system built on the Yelp Open Dataset, combining traditional machine learning, deep learning, and generative AI to predict ratings, classify sentiment, and extract fine-grained aspect-based insights from millions of customer reviews.

Research Context

Online review platforms like Yelp fundamentally shape consumer decisions. Understanding not just whether a review is positive or negative, but specifically what aspects customers appreciate or criticize (food quality, service, ambiance, price) provides invaluable business intelligence.

This project explores the full spectrum of NLP approaches from classical methods to cutting-edge transformer models and LLM-based reasoning.

Phase 1: Exploratory Data Analysis

Conducted comprehensive analysis across multiple Yelp dataset files (reviews, business, users, photos) to uncover patterns:

  • Review distribution by category, business popularity, and reviewer experience
  • Correlation between review length, sentiment polarity, and rating scores
  • Vocabulary analysis comparing negative vs. positive reviews using TF-IDF
  • Impact of photos on business ratings and review engagement
  • Reviewer behavior patterns (power users vs. casual reviewers)

Phase 2: Predictive Modeling

Task 1: Sentiment Classification (Positive/Negative/Neutral)

Implemented and compared multiple text representation approaches:

  • Bag-of-Words: Baseline with logistic regression and SVM
  • TF-IDF: Weighted term importance for better discrimination
  • BERT Embeddings: Contextual representations capturing semantic nuances
  • Deep Learning: MLP and CNN architectures on dense embeddings
  • Transformers: Fine-tuned pre-trained models (BERT, RoBERTa)

Task 2: Rating Prediction (1-5 Stars)

Extended classification models to multi-class regression, predicting exact star ratings from review text with evaluation on MSE and accuracy metrics.

Phase 3: Generative AI Integration

Zero-Shot and Few-Shot Classification:

Leveraged large language models to classify sentiment without explicit training data, comparing performance against supervised approaches and analyzing prompt engineering strategies.

Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis (ABSA):

Implemented structured LLM pipelines using LangChain to extract granular insights:

  • Identify mentioned aspects (food, service, ambiance, price, cleanliness)
  • Classify sentiment per aspect (positive/negative/neutral)
  • Generate structured JSON output for downstream analysis
  • Chain reasoning steps for complex multi-aspect reviews

Example output: A review might be positive about food quality but negative about service speed, providing actionable business intelligence impossible to capture with simple sentiment scores.

Technical Implementation

  • Data Pipeline: Efficient preprocessing of multi-GB Yelp datasets with pandas and distributed processing
  • Model Training: PyTorch-based training loops with GPU acceleration, checkpointing, and early stopping
  • Hyperparameter Tuning: Grid search and Bayesian optimization for model selection
  • Evaluation Framework: Comprehensive metrics (accuracy, F1, precision, recall, confusion matrices)
  • LLM Integration: Structured prompts with LangChain for reliable aspect extraction

Key Findings & Results

  • Transformer models (fine-tuned BERT) significantly outperformed classical ML approaches
  • TF-IDF with SVM provided strong baseline with much faster inference
  • Zero-shot LLM classification achieved competitive accuracy without training data
  • Aspect-based analysis revealed nuanced sentiment patterns invisible to aggregate scores
  • Review length strongly correlates with negative sentiment (detailed complaints)
  • Power reviewers tend toward more critical evaluations

Business Applications

This system enables businesses to:

  • Monitor sentiment trends across thousands of reviews automatically
  • Identify specific operational issues (e.g., "service is slow" vs. "food quality declined")
  • Benchmark performance against competitors on granular aspects
  • Prioritize improvements based on aspect-sentiment distributions
  • Detect emerging issues before they impact overall ratings
